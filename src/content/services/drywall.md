## Common Drywall Issues We Fix

Drywall damage happens. Accidents, settling, water leaks, wear and tear. Here's what we see most often in Charlotte homes.

### Holes and Cracks

**Small holes (nail/screw holes):**  
Left behind from picture frames, shelves, or TVs. Quick to patch, but need proper filling and sanding to disappear completely.

**Medium holes (doorknob, accidental impact):**  
The classic doorknob-through-the-wall. Or moving furniture that got too close. These need backing support, not just spackle.

**Large holes (furniture, impact damage):**  
Bigger damage from furniture, kids, or accidents. Requires cutting out the damaged section, installing backing, and patching with new drywall.

**Cracks (settling, foundation movement):**  
Common in Charlotte's older brick homes. Foundation settling creates stress cracks, especially around doors and windows. If your home was built in the 1950s-70s in Myers Park, Dilworth, or Plaza Midwood, you've probably seen these. We repair the crack and reinforce with mesh tape to prevent it from reopening.

### Water Damage

Charlotte's humidity doesn't mess around. Water damage spreads fast here.

**Ceiling stains from leaks:**  
That brown spot on your ceiling? It's from a roof leak, upstairs plumbing, or AC condensation. Even if the leak is fixed, the stained drywall needs replacementâ€”it's compromised and likely holds mold.

**Warped or soft drywall:**  
If you press on the drywall and it feels soft or spongy, the water has soaked through. It won't dry properly. That section needs to be cut out and replaced.

**Mold potential (Charlotte's 80%+ summer humidity):**  
Water damage + Charlotte humidity = mold growth in 24-48 hours. If there's visible mold or a musty smell, we remove the affected drywall completely, dry the cavity, and install fresh material.

**Complete section replacement:**  
Sometimes a small leak damages a large area. We don't just patch around itâ€”we replace the entire section for structural integrity and a clean finish.

### Texture Matching

**This is where most DIY repairs fail.** You can fill a hole. But making the repair invisible? That's art + experience.

**Orange peel texture:**  
The most common in Charlotte homes. Looks like the surface of an orange. Requires a sprayer and the right viscosity mix to replicate perfectly.

**Knockdown texture:**  
Popular in newer Charlotte homes. The texture is sprayed, then lightly "knocked down" with a trowel. Timing and technique matterâ€”too early or too late and it won't match.

**Smooth finish:**  
Common in older homes and high-end remodels. No texture, just perfectly smooth. Requires multiple coats of joint compound, sanding between each, and a steady hand.

**The challenge:**  
Walk into any Home Depot and ask for "texture spray." They'll sell it to you. But matching your home's exact textureâ€”the pattern, the depth, the consistencyâ€”that's not DIY territory. **We bring samples, test on scrap, and dial it in before touching your wall.**

### Other Common Issues

**Nail pops:**  
Nails backing out over time, creating small bumps. Common as houses settle. We reset the nail, add a screw nearby for support, fill, sand, and paint.

**Corner bead damage:**  
The metal corner protectors on outside corners get dinged by vacuum cleaners, furniture, kids. We replace the damaged section and blend it seamlessly.

**Tape separation:**  
The tape between drywall sheets pulls away, creating visible seams or cracks. We re-tape with mesh or paper tape (depending on the area), apply fresh joint compound, and refinish.

**Poor previous repairs:**  
We see a lot of "my husband tried to fix it" patches. No judgmentâ€”drywall is harder than it looks. We redo it properly so it actually disappears.
