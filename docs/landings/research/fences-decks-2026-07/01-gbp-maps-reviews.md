## 2. Negative Complaint Patterns (ranked by frequency)

### 2.1 No-shows, ghosting, and unreturned calls (MOST COMMON, cross-category)
The single most repeated theme. Customers routinely say they called multiple companies and got no reply, or were stood up on a scheduled date.

- "I initially contacted two other fence companies with no reply." — Chris E, on 76 Fence's review page (76fence.com)
- Superior Fence's own Waxhaw page markets around this: "after another company didn't show as agreed" the customer switched to them (superiorfenceandrail.com).
- Superior Fence BBB complaint: the manager "NO CALLED/NO SHOWED the date they planned" for requested fence fixes (bbb.org, Concord NC profile).
- Archadeck: "Many customers reported not getting calls back, or being told things would be taken care of but they were not" (Angi summary).

### 2.2 Warranty refusal / won't come back to fix defects
Several competitors either don't offer warranties at all or drag their feet on repairs.

- Champion Fence (Birdeye): "We paid thousands of dollars and waited many many months for this fence to be installed incorrectly in unprofessionally. They will not fix the gaps or the inconsistency in the very expensive fence."
- Archadeck: warranty response after Hurricane Florence was "disappointing" and "they won't stand behind the work they were paid well to do" (Yelp/Angi).
- Charlotte Decks & Porches "does not offer warranties." The Deck Builder warranty is limited to "certain composite decking and craftsmanship" only.
- Superior Fence BBB: promised cedar-picket repairs went "Six months" with "still no response."

### 2.3 Timeline slippage / "took forever" (especially DECKS)
Deck projects blow past quoted timelines by weeks to months.

- Archadeck: "An 8 week project took 12 weeks," first half "in SLOW motion."
- Archadeck: quoted "5 and 8 days," actually "started on December 16 and was not finished until February 14" (60 days).
- Archadeck: project "scheduled to start in November but wasn't started until mid-December and not fully completed until mid-February."
- Charlotte deck contractor (Porch/Yelp): "cuts corners at every possible opportunity" after "4 months on a project estimated to take one month."

### 2.4 Unfinished work invoiced as complete
Billing ahead of completion is a specific, quotable trust breaker.

- Archadeck: a customer "was invoiced for 'Deck Completion' two weeks before the deck was actually complete, with no stairs, unfinished railings, and unfinished deck skirt" (Angi).
- Archadeck: "demo materials weren't removed when promised and required multiple follow-ups."

### 2.5 Poor workmanship / gaps / has to be redone by someone else
- Champion Fence: "gaps" and "inconsistency" in an expensive fence, unfixed.
- Ugo Construction: "They completely messed up my pool deck and now I'm having a second company come out and fix what they couldn't do."
- Superior Fence BBB: "poor door install and cracked/discolored/poor quality wood boards"; also "did not install the fence from property marker to property marker" and "damaged a sprinkler head."

### 2.6 Rude / high-pressure or dismissive sales
- Archadeck (Yelp): "Easily the rudest sales call I've ever been a part of -- and I'm in sales myself. Budget was never the issue, yet I was treated like I was wasting his time from the moment the conversation started."

### 2.7 Over-budget / surprise pricing
- Archadeck (Yelp): a rep "came $30k over the budget that was given."

### 2.8 Materials mismanagement
- Archadeck: "Running out of materials (or having excess materials) became an all too common theme."

### 2.9 Deposit-then-ghost (industry-wide caution, adjacent market)
Not confirmed against the specific South Charlotte named competitors, but a live regional pattern:
- Charleston SC (Live 5 News): customer paid "$700 deposit via Cash App," got "excuses about car trouble and a broken phone," then "stopped hearing from the contractor"; no license on file. Illustrates why "one-third up front, balance on satisfactory completion" reassurance converts.

## 4. Fence-specific vs Deck-specific Patterns

### Fence-specific
- Wins: same-day/fast quotes, one-day installs, communication, clean lines, care around trees and slopes/drop-offs, gate function.
- Complaints: install not run "property marker to property marker," gaps/inconsistency in panels, damaged irrigation (sprinkler heads), gate/door install problems, warranty/repair foot-dragging, no-show on scheduled fix dates.
- Language to echo: "installed in one day," "straight lines even on a slope," "we mark and confirm your property line," "gates that latch right."

### Deck-specific
- Wins: fast fair quote, hitting the start date, handling hidden rot at the ledger/house connection well, warranty on composite + craftsmanship.
- Complaints: severe timeline slip (weeks to months), invoiced before stairs/railings/skirt are done, corner-cutting, materials running out mid-job, warranty refusal after storms, "no warranty offered" at all, builder-grade rot (posts/spindles) that original builder won't fix.
- Language to echo: "we finish the stairs, railings, and skirting before we call it done," "we handle the rotten boards at the house," "written timeline you can hold us to."
- Demand signal: Eastwood Homes builder-deck complaints (rotting support posts, leaning post, falling-apart spindles/railings) = strong repair/replace lead pool in newer South Charlotte subdivisions.

## 6. Top 5 Market Gaps iFIXX Can Exploit

1. **Answer the phone and show up.** The most common complaint across every competitor is no-reply and no-show. A simple, credible promise ("we return every call same day and we keep the appointment") directly attacks the market's biggest failure. Winners (76 Fence, Top Rail) already prove responsiveness sells.

2. **Stand behind the work / clear written warranty on craftsmanship.** Competitors either "do not offer warranties" (Charlotte Decks & Porches), limit them narrowly (The Deck Builder), or refuse to honor them (Champion, Archadeck, Superior). A plain, insured craftsmanship guarantee is a wedge. Do not overpromise materials warranties you cannot back.

3. **Finish before you bill / no "completion" invoice on an unfinished deck.** Archadeck's invoice-before-stairs complaint is vivid and repeatable. iFIXX can promise: stairs, railings, and skirting done before final payment. Pairs with the standard "deposit up front, balance on satisfactory completion" reassurance that counters the deposit-ghost fear.

4. **Honest, held-to timelines (decks especially).** "8 weeks became 12," "one month became four." A written schedule with realistic dates, and proactive updates, beats the franchise slip-and-excuse pattern.

5. **Clean, correct, property-line-accurate installs by our own insured crews (not mystery subs).** Sprinkler damage, wrong property lines, and gaps come from sloppy or subbed-out labor. Position iFIXX crews as careful (mark the line, protect irrigation, straight runs on slopes) and accountable, contrasting the "second company had to fix it" stories.

Bonus adjacent gap: **builder-grade deck rot repair** (Eastwood-type subdivisions) is an under-served, high-intent repair niche competitors treat as too small or ignore, yet it fits qualified homeowner projects.
