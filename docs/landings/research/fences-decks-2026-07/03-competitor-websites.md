# Competitor Website Analysis: Fences & Decks (South Charlotte)

> Research date: 2026-07-01
> Scope: Fence and deck contractor landing pages for iFIXX expansion into South Charlotte (Ballantyne, Waxhaw, Indian Land SC, Fort Mill SC, Pineville, SouthPark, Matthews, Weddington)
> Method: WebSearch to find real competitors, WebFetch to read their live home/service pages. 13 companies analyzed: local Charlotte/SC, reference markets (Raleigh, Atlanta), and one national franchise.
> Purpose: Reverse-engineer their landing-page and conversion playbook so iFIXX can differentiate.

## 2. Deep Notes Per Competitor

### Local Charlotte / South Charlotte

**Carolina Deck Pros** — Positioning is soft and generic ("outdoor paradise"). Notably thin on proof: no years, badges, warranty, or insurance claims on the page. Their one differentiator is flexible consult channels (phone, FaceTime/Zoom, or in person) and a seasonal urgency line ("Booking now for Spring and Summer 2026"). Splits work into new build and "repair and restore your existing deck." Friction: no visible instant form; relies on a "Contact Us" link.

**James Fence & Gate** — The only local player that genuinely bridges fence AND deck (deck/porch/stair handrails), which maps closely to iFIXX's handyman-adjacent positioning. H1 is a question stack. Strong trust cluster: "over 20 years experience," "Licensed & Insured," "Guaranteed Workmanship," "Family Owned & Operated," "Same Day Service," "We Recycle & Donate Locally." Financing via GreenSky. "No job too big or too small" — the opposite of iFIXX's "qualified projects, not tiny jobs" stance. Form has a CAPTCHA (friction).

**FencePro LLC (ncscfencepro.com)** — Cleanest local fence positioning: "Strong, Beautiful Fences Built for Local Homes." Explicitly splits "Installation. Repairs. Refurbishing." — the clearest repair callout among locals. Proof is specific and modest: "30+ years," "4.9-star rating from more than 41 happy customers." 5-field contact form (First, Last, Email, Phone, Message). No pricing or financing.

**B Line Fencing** — Broad material menu (aluminum, vinyl, chain link, wood, ranch rail, pool code, handrails, electric gates, commercial). Install-heavy; repair only surfaces inside a customer review. Proof is light (15+ yrs, BBB, 5 testimonials). Form is comprehensive: Name, Phone, Email, Address, **Service Interest dropdown**, Message. No pricing/financing.

**Mighty Oak Fence** — Weakest CTA of the set ("Learn More" pointing to About Us instead of a quote). Good credential stack for SC (Licensed & Insured, Fence Workers Association member, BBB A+, 1-Year Warranty, York County Chamber). No years-in-business, no pricing.

**Archadeck of Charlotte** — The franchise benchmark and the clearest "what iFIXX should NOT be." Luxury design-build: "Premier Outdoor Living Space Designer and Builder," decks **from $15,000**, porches **from $40,000**. CTA "Instant Free Estimate" but the real motion is a complimentary in-home **design consultation with 3D rendering**. Heavy proof: Since 1988, Google 5.0/4.6, AZEK/TimberTech/BBB/HomeAdvisor badges, "trained, licensed and certified," "the most expansive customer warranty in the industry." Form asks First, Last, Email, Phone, Address, ZIP.

**Green Exterior Remodeling** — Premium local, certification-led: "Charlotte's Only TrexPRO® Platinum Certified Deck Builder." Explicit permitting claim ("Permitting & Site Prep" as step 3) and "fully licensed and insured in both NC and SC." Warranties up to 50 years + Lifetime Workmanship. Transparent pricing ($25–$80/sq ft; 300 sq ft ~ $8k–$12k; multi-level $20k–$45k+). Rich form including a **Financing interest (Yes/No)** toggle and project-type dropdown. This is the fully-loaded design-build playbook.

**Deck Plus** — "Charlotte's #1 Deck Builder." Publishes actual **NC (84085) and SC (G 123156) license numbers** — a strong trust move iFIXX cannot copy (iFIXX uses "insured," never "licensed"). Google 4.9 / 100+ reviews, 15+ yrs, Trex/BBB/Angi/Houzz logos. 6-field form (First, Last, Email, Phone, ZIP, Service dropdown + opt-in). Focuses on new build; no repair split.

**Charlotte Deck Repair** — The closest philosophical match to iFIXX among locals, and instructive. Despite the domain, the H1 leads with "Deck Builders … Custom, Durable & Affordable." Repair is a clear category ("Deck Repair & Restoration – Bring Your Deck Back to Life"). Two things worth copying: **photo upload in the form**, and **transparent low-end pricing** ("A small deck starts at $2,500, while larger, custom decks can be $10,000 or more"). Weakness: zero license/insurance/warranty proof — a credibility gap iFIXX can beat with "insured" + reviews.

### Reference markets

**Hawk Fences of Raleigh** — Best-in-class conversion machine and the strongest pattern to copy (minus "Licensed"). H1 "#1 Top-Rated Fence Company In Raleigh, NC." **AI-powered virtual instant estimator** is the hero CTA ("Get an Instant Estimate"). Clean 3-step process: Virtual Estimate → Consultation Call → Installation. Install-only; no repair. This is the call-center/franchise feel iFIXX must avoid.

**Outback Deck (Atlanta)** — Great in-home sales mechanics: "**On-the-Spot Pricing**" and "**Quotes Good For One Year**" remove the "we'll get back to you" friction. Veteran-owned since 2010, multi-platform review stack (Google 4.7 / Angi 4.9 / Houzz 4.5 / GuildQuality 4.9), "Built-Safe Guarantee," $750-off promo, financing. Does full teardown/rebuild — a repair-adjacent angle worth noting.

**Perimeter / Kirchner (Atlanta, context)** — Reference for financing framing: "6- or 12-month interest-free financing," named partners (Acorn, HFS). Design-build, in-home consult.

## 3. Table Stakes (What Everyone Says / Does)

These appear so consistently that their absence reads as a red flag. iFIXX must match all of them:

- **"Free estimate / free consultation"** — universal. Every single company leads with it.
- **A single phone number, repeated** (Charlotte Deck Repair repeats theirs 10+ times). iFIXX: (980) 391-6833.
- **Years in business** — 15 to 35+ yrs is the norm (only Carolina Deck Pros and Charlotte Deck Repair omit it, and they look weaker for it).
- **Star rating + review count** — Google 4.7–5.0 with a specific number (41, 100+, 160+, 1,460+). Specific counts beat vague "5-star."
- **Trust badges** — BBB is near-universal; then Google, Angi/Angie's List, Houzz, HomeAdvisor. Deck players add manufacturer badges (Trex, TimberTech, AZEK).
- **"Insured"** (and, for most, "Licensed") — standard credibility line.
- **Material menu / service grid** — fence: wood, vinyl, aluminum, chain link, privacy; deck: composite/Trex vs wood.
- **Local place-name lists** — everyone lists the exact suburbs they serve (Ballantyne, Waxhaw, Fort Mill, Indian Land, Matthews, etc.). Strong for local SEO and relevance.
- **Family-owned / veteran-owned / local** identity signal.
- **A simple lead form**: First, Last, Email, Phone, + service dropdown or message. 5–7 fields is standard.
- **Warranty language** — 1-year workmanship is the floor; lifetime workmanship is the premium flex.

## 4. White Space (What Nobody Says — iFIXX Opportunities)

### Fence white space
- **Repair as a headline, not a footnote.** Nearly every fence company (B Line, Mighty Oak, Stand Strong, James) leads with installation and buries repair. Only FencePro even lists "Repairs. Refurbishing." iFIXX can own **"Fence repair and replacement done right"** — leaning-post fixes, storm damage, replacing sections, re-hanging gates — as a first-class, headlined service. This matches iFIXX's handyman DNA and is genuinely underserved.
- **Honest scope + fast turnaround for mid-size jobs.** Franchises push design; nobody speaks plainly to the homeowner who has a sagging gate or a 40-foot run to replace and just wants a fair, quick fix.
- **Gate specialization** (sticking, sagging, dragging, hardware) is mentioned by no one as a lead service.

### Deck white space
- **Deck repair, board replacement, and re-boarding as the lead** — not $15k–$40k rebuilds. Every serious Charlotte deck site (Green Exterior, Deck Plus, Archadeck) is design-build and prices from $8k–$15k+. Charlotte Deck Repair is the only one leaning repair, and it has weak proof. iFIXX can own **"Deck repair, board and railing replacement, and safe rebuilds"** for homeowners whose deck is aging but not ready for a luxury teardown.
- **Safety framing.** Loose railings, soft/rotted boards, wobbly stairs, popped fasteners — nobody frames deck work around "make your deck safe again." That is an honest, non-luxury angle iFIXX can own.
- **Refinishing / staining / sealing** as a standalone entry service (Carolina Deck Pros lists it in passing; no one leads with it).

### Cross-cutting white space
- **Fence AND deck under one honest, insured local crew** — only James Fence bridges both, and awkwardly. iFIXX's handyman positioning makes "one call for fence or deck, install or repair" natural.
- **Transparent, plain-language "what a fair job costs"** without the luxury framing. Charlotte Deck Repair ($2,500 start) and Green Exterior ($/sq ft) hint at it; most hide price. iFIXX can be the fair/practical/honest voice.
- **Fast, real response** (not "we'll schedule a designer"). Nobody local promises a quick human callback for a mid-size job.

## 5. Form & CTA Patterns Worth Copying

**Field counts.** The winners keep it to **5–7 fields**. Standard set: First Name, Last Name, Email, Phone, + ZIP and/or a Service dropdown. iFIXX QuickEstimate (Name, Phone, Service) is already lean — keep it that way for the primary form; a ZIP field improves routing and local relevance with near-zero friction.

**"Build or repair" split in the form.** B Line and Green Exterior use a **Service Interest dropdown**. iFIXX should include a dropdown with clear split: *Fence — install/replace*, *Fence — repair*, *Deck — build/rebuild*, *Deck — repair*, *Not sure*. This qualifies the lead and routes it (and quietly screens out tiny jobs).

**Photo upload.** Charlotte Deck Repair lets homeowners **attach a photo**. For repair-led positioning this is a strong differentiator — a photo of a sagging fence or rotted board lets iFIXX give a faster, more accurate response and signals "we actually fix things." Recommend adding optional photo upload to the fence/deck form.

**Instant/virtual estimate CTA.** Hawk Fences ("Get an Instant Estimate," AI estimator) and Stand Strong ("Virtual Fence Estimator") convert on the promise of speed. iFIXX does not need an AI calculator, but the **CTA language of speed** ("Get a Fast Free Estimate," "Send a Photo, Get a Quick Quote") is worth adopting.

**Quote validity / no-pressure framing.** Outback Deck's "Quotes Good For One Year" and "On-the-Spot Pricing" reduce commitment anxiety. An honest, low-pressure line fits iFIXX's voice.

**CTA hierarchy that works:** Primary = Request Free Estimate (form); Secondary = Call Now (single number). This matches iFIXX's existing CTA system — no change needed, just apply it to fence/deck pages. Avoid weak CTAs like Mighty Oak's "Learn More."

**Proof density near the form.** The strongest pages (Hawk, Green Exterior) stack rating + review count + years + insured + warranty within eyeshot of the CTA. iFIXX should cluster: star rating + specific review count + "Insured" + years serving South Charlotte + a workmanship guarantee.

## 6. Claims to AVOID (and Why)

| Claim competitors make | Who | Why iFIXX must AVOID it |
|---|---|---|
| **"Licensed"** / published license numbers | Deck Plus (NC 84085, SC G 123156), Archadeck, Green Exterior, Hawk, James, Mighty Oak | iFIXX hard rule: say **"Insured," NEVER "Licensed."** Do not publish or imply a contractor license. Lead with "Insured" + reviews + workmanship guarantee instead. |
| **"Permitting handled" / "we pull the permits"** | Green Exterior ("Permitting & Site Prep"), franchises imply it | iFIXX hard rule: do NOT promise permits are handled. Keep scope to the build/repair work itself. |
| **Luxury design-build positioning** ("Premier Outdoor Living Designer," 3D renderings, "outdoor paradise") | Archadeck, Green Exterior, Deck Plus, Outback | iFIXX is not luxury/design-build. Positioning is fair/practical/honest/local/efficient. Luxury framing invites $15k+ price expectations and the wrong leads. |
| **Lifetime / 50-year guarantees** | Stand Strong, Hawk, Green Exterior, Archadeck ("most expansive warranty in the industry") | Overpromising. iFIXX should offer an honest, defined **workmanship guarantee**, not "lifetime." Manufacturer material warranties can be mentioned factually, but don't headline a lifetime promise. |
| **National/franchise / "Family of Brands" / press-logo** signals | Stand Strong, Archadeck | iFIXX is local, not a franchise or call-center. Keep the voice human and Charlotte-specific. |
| **"No job too big or too small" / cheapest** | James Fence ("no job too small"), Charlotte Deck Repair ("Affordable"), Outback ("price you can afford") | iFIXX targets **qualified projects, not tiny jobs**, and is NOT the "cheapest." Use fair/practical/honest, and let the service dropdown screen out tiny jobs. |
| Em-dashes / en-dashes in copy | (formatting) | iFIXX hard rule: never use — or – in marketing copy. Use periods or "and." |

**Also note:** iFIXX must use the single phone number **(980) 391-6833** everywhere (competitors repeat their number heavily — copy the repetition, not their numbers).

## 7. Confidence Notes

- **High confidence:** H1s, CTAs, and trust elements are quoted directly from live pages fetched 2026-07-01. Pricing figures (Archadeck $15k+, Green Exterior $/sq ft, Charlotte Deck Repair $2,500, Hawk $89/mo) are verbatim from the pages.
- **Medium confidence:** Form field lists reflect what each page exposed to the fetch; some sites gate full forms behind JS or a separate contact page, so field counts may be slightly incomplete (e.g., Mighty Oak, Stand Strong forms were on linked pages, not the homepage).
- **Lower confidence / not independently verified:** Review counts and star ratings are the companies' self-reported figures, not checked against Google/BBB directly. Franchise warranty terms ("most expansive in the industry") are marketing claims.
- **Sample composition:** 9 local (Charlotte/SC), 3 reference-market (Raleigh Hawk, Atlanta Outback, Atlanta Perimeter/Kirchner as context), 1 national franchise (Stand Strong). Good coverage of both fence-led and deck-led players; the fence side skews SC (Fort Mill/Indian Land), the deck side skews Charlotte proper.
- **Gap:** No pure South-Charlotte-only repair specialist exists in the sample. That absence is itself the finding: the repair-led, insured-not-licensed, non-luxury lane is genuinely open.
